Benefits of Honey

What makes Honey special?

Honey has tremendous Health benefits!

Antiseptics

Honey contains antiseptics that have been proven to reduce stroke risk.

Helps Infections

Honey has antibacterical properties and modern medical science has managed to find use for honey in chronic wound managment and combating infection. Honey can sterilize wounds and promote healing, and also reduce pain, odor, and wound size.It can also treat antibiotic-resistant bacteria and long-term ulcers and wounds after surgery and from burns.

Heart

Honey is healthy for your heart and can protect you from heart disease with improving cholesterol levels.

Fertility

Honey has been hyped for its potential to boost fertility in both men and women.

Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a common uncurable skin condition that causes redness, blistering, itching. Studies have shown that using a mixture of honey, olive oil and beeswax has helped reduction of redness and more for patients.

Blood Sugar Regulation

With a low glycemic index, it is a good substitute for sugar and can help you monitor blood sugar

Interesting Facts about Honey

  1. A typical beehive can produce anywhere from 30 to 100 pounds of honey a year. To produce a single pound of honey, a colony of bees must collect nectar from approximately two million flowers and fly over 55,000 miles. This amounts to an entire lifetime of work for around 800 bees.
  2. In 11th century Germany, honey was so highly valued for its beer-sweetening abilities that German feudal lords required their peasants to make them payments of honey and beeswax.
  3. Bees work hard all summer to ensure they will have enough honey to sustain the hive through the winter. During the colder months, bees occupy their time by clustering themselves around the queen and shivering their bodies to fill the hive with warmth. All that shivering burns a lot of calories, so honey makes for the perfect high-energy diet.
