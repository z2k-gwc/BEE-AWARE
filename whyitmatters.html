Why Plants Need Pollination 

     Plants need pollinators for a number of
    reasons. Pollinating animals travel from
    plant to plant carrying pollen on their bodies
    for a vital interaction.This interaction allows
    the transfer of genetic material crucial to the
    reproductive systems of most flowering plants.
    Pollinators are responsible for providing 1 out
    of 3 bites of food.

What a World Without Bees Looks Like

Save the Bees

The most important thing that bees do is pollinate.The vast majority of plants we need for food rely on pollination. Almonds and vanilla and apples and squash all need bees to pollinate! If we lose bees, all levels of the food chain will suffer since we will be losing our base: plants.

Save the Bees Planet Bee Honeypedia Greenpeace

5 ways to Save the Bees:

  1. Plant bee friendly plants, flowers and food.
  2. Avoid the use of chemicals and pesticides in your garden.
  3. Support local sustainable farmers.
  4. Buy local raw honey
  5. Stop local gardens and stores from selling bee killing pesticides. One way to do this is to decrease the demand by not buying the product.
